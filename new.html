Why IEEE Sections Must Follow IEEE Branding Guidelines

Vijay S Paul, Chair
IEEE R10 Information Management Committee

As an organization with thousands of members across different regions, maintaining a consistent and professional brand identity is essential. IEEE has developed branding guidelines that all Sections and organizational units must follow. These ensure a unified presence using standard logos, fonts like Formata/Calibri, and official color schemes.

Brand consistency strengthens recognition, enhances professionalism, and improves engagement. When all Sections adhere to branding standards, IEEE is better positioned as a trusted global leader in technology. Refer to the IEEE Brand Identity Guidelines to ensure your communications reflect our shared standards.

Awards Presentation Ceremony at the IEEE R10 Meeting at Incheon

During the IEEE R10 Annual General Meeting banquet at Incheon on 22 February 2025, the Awards and Recognition Committee (ARC) organized an Awards Presentation Ceremony to honor exceptional contributions. Twenty-five 2024 Outstanding Section and Volunteer Awards, two Banners, and the 2024 IEEE MGA Award-R10 were presented by IEEE leaders including President Dr. Kathleen Kramer and R10 Director Prof. Takako Hashimoto.

The award winners hailed from across Region 10, including India, China, Japan, Korea, Pakistan, Malaysia, and Sri Lanka. Prof. Chris Lee, Chair of the IEEE R10 ARC, served as the Emcee of this celebratory evening that recognized dedication, excellence, and leadership within the IEEE community.
